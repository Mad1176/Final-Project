Welcome!

This website is designed to give visitors an overview of some of the more popular trail networks in the Upper Valley. Each page will contain information about the type of trails in the network, navigation to the trailhead(s), what sort of gear to bring, suggested routes, and food and accommodation recommendations. This will help people who are new or unfamiliar with the area find fun places to go mountain biking!

Trails in the Upper Valley

The Upper Valley is a great place to go mountain biking as it has a very diverse and fun offering of trail networks. There are so many trails that it is not uncommon for towns to have multiple unique trail networks. For this website, the term “Upper Valley” refers to the area of Vermont and New Hampshire spanning the I-91 corridor from Windsor, Vermont up to Lebanon, New Hampshire. The trails in the Upper Valley are generally known for being relatively technical and having a lot of elevation gain and while this may be true for some trail systems, with the right information it is possible to find smoother, more beginner-friendly terrain with less elevation. Most trail networks mentioned in this website have decent infrastructure, which includes designated parking lots, trail maps, and usually bathrooms and changing facilities. In addition to this, all of these trail networks are on Trailforks (a navigation application for MTB and hiking trails available for both Android and Apple). This website will help visitors find the trail network that is the right fit for them and prepare them for what to expect when they hit the trails. The trail networks that this webiste will cover are as follows:

  • Ascutney Trails
  • Moody Park
  • Arrowhead
  • Boston Lot
  • Aqueduct Trails and Mount Peg
  • Saskadena Six

Events

There are many MTB related events and races held in the Upper Valley. These are fun for mountain bikers of any level and most of these can be also enjoyed by members of the community and not just cyclists.

Vermont 50
The Vermont 50 is a 50-mile MTB and running race held at Ascutney Trails in late September. Although the race starts at Ascutney Trails, the course takes racers all the way to Woodstock and back, treating them to iconic scenery and trails along the way. It's fun for spectators as well, as the venue offers live music, food, and vendors.
The Vermont 50
Vermont Overland
The Vermont Overland is a gravel race/ride also hosted by Ascutney Trails and Ascutney Outdoors. This event takes riders through the scenic gravel roads in the area and is a fun event for any mountain biker who has a gravel bike. This event also offers great live music, food, and even its own signature beer for both competitors and spectators.
The Vermont Overland
Flow State Vermont
The Flow State festival is an MTB festival at Ascutney Trails in late July. This event has guided rides, demo rides, vendors from the cycling and outdoor industries, live music, food, and a beer garden. This is a super fun event for any mountain biker in New England!
Flow State Vermont
ESC Enduro at Arrowhead
This enduro race is hosted at Arrowhead in early September and is a fun event for both racers and spectators. Racers get to tackle the steep and technical trails that Arrowhead gets to offer. This is a very fun stop of the Maxxis ESC race series.
ESC Arrowhead
Tuesday Night Ride (TNR)
TNR is a weekly group ride hosted by the members of Ascutney Trails. Every Tuesday night at 5:45 they go for a group ride. These rides alternate between Ascutney Trails and other networks in the Upper Valley and are broken into differently paced groups. These rides are open to all abilities and are a great way to experience the different trail networks in the Upper Valley.
Tuesday Night Rides

Local Bike Shops

The Upper Valley has multiple bike shops that keep riders on the trails, offer upgrades and new bikes, help build and maintain their local trails and are happy to just talk about bikes are riding with anyone. The Wheel House in Claremont, NH is a great shop that has a great inventory of Trek and Revel bikes and a modern service area with knowledgeable technicians. They are happy to help and work on any type of bike. Mason Racing is a Rocky Mountain dealer in Lebanon, NH with great service and a mountain bike oriented shop. The Boot Pro is the area's newest shop, they are a Specialized dealer and also an excellent ski shop. The owners are passionate mountain bikers are always volunteering at their local networks.
