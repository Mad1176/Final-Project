Ascutney Trails

Ascutney Trails is one of the Upper Valley's largest trail networks with upwards of 30 miles of singletrack and is constantly expanding. The trails in this network have a reputation of being tight, technical and climb-heavy, while this is true for a decent portion of the trails here it is possible to find smoother trails here and a few select loops with minimal elevation. They have also added a flow trail and a skills park built by Powder Horn Trail Company. Ascutney Trails has three different trailheads with different types of riding, however all of the trails can be accessed from all of the trailheads.

449 Ski Tow Road, Brownsville, VT 05037
Ascutney Outdoors Center is the main trailhead with the largest parking lot, most infastruce and quickest access to the flow trail and skill park. This trailhead has the best access to their intermediate trails.
881 Coaching Lane, Brownsville, VT 05037
The Town Forest trail head is has a smaller parking with a small changing room. It offers the quickest access to Ascutney's most technical trails.
1826 Back Mountain Road, Windsor, VT 05089
This trailhead at the Ascutney state park offers access to Swoops and Loops as well as the Weathersfield Town forest. It is also possible to get to and from the main trails to the state park via the Norcross Trail.

What to bring?

While you can ride these trails on any mountain bike, a cross country bike is recommended for most trails as you will probably be doing a lot of climbing! If you are planning on just sessioning the skills park a larger travel trail bike or dirt jumper would be better suited for the larger jumps. Given the amount of trails in this network it is recommend that you bring food, water and tools in case of a mechanical.

Beginner Loop

The most beginner friendly trails will be found at the state park trailhead. The recommended loop is to start at the trailhead and ride Swoops and Loops out of the side of the road where the state park entrance is, and then come back on the opposing side of the road and when you return to the car you can cross the road again to ride Rick's Road to Cloudspin and then return to the car back on Rick's Road. This loop would be roughly 6 miles on smooth trails with minimal elevation.

Suggested Advanced and Intermediate Trails

Gracie's Loop is an iconic intermediate Ascutney trail in the center of the network, it can be ridden both ways and is usually part of any ride between the main parking lot and the town forest. The Norcross Trail connects the main trailhead to the state park entrance and is a fun traverse across the mountain. It is considered an intermediate trail but does have some technical spots and it is recommended to shuttle unless you want a really big ride. Paradise Hill is a fun intermediate descent accessed from the Grassy Knoll, it combines the tight and technical riding Ascutney is known for with large, handbuilt berms. Both Ridgeback and Jim's Jam are regarded as some of the more technical trails in the network, they are most easily accessed from the Town Forest entrance.These trails contain tight turns, technical climbs and, large rock rolls.

Food and Lodging

The Brownsville Butcher and Pantry is within riding distance from the main trailhead. They serve breakfast, lunch, and dinner, and have a full grocery with a wide selection of beer and wine, both for sale and consumption. The menu and hours can be found on their website. Both the Ascutney State Park and Running Bear Campground are good options for camping. The Ascutney Mountain Resort is the closest option for those looking a decent hotel. It should be noted that camping is not permitted at any of the trailheads.
