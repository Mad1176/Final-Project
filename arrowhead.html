Arrowhead Recreation Area

Arrowhead's trails have recently been revamped by volunteers from the Wheelhouse bike shop. They have created a network of fun and challenging downhill trails that host a round of the Maxxis ESC Enduro in the early fall. These trails are enjoyed by enduro and downhill riders throughout New England. The trails are Arrowhead are known for being short, steep and filled with techncal features such as drops, jumps and rollers. The gravel climbing trail is currently a multi-use trail and the recration area does have a few atv trails but all of the downhill bike trails are one directoion and bike-only. The only trailhead is at 18 Robert Easter Way, Claremont, NH 03743 and is also the parking lot of the Arrowhead Recrational Area tubing and ski rope tow.

What to bring?

Arrowhead probably contains some of the most technical and high-risk trails out of all the networks on this website. A cross-country bike is not recommended for this network, riders should have a long-travel bike such as an enduro or downhill bike. Full-face helmets and knee pads are recommended for this trail network as well. However, since the runs are short here it is not nessicary to bring tools or a flat kit with you as you're always a short walk from the car. Water and snacks are optional to bring on the bike but it's never a bad idea to keep some in the car in case you get hungry in between runs.

Reccomended Trails

All of the trails on Arrowhead are accessed via the gravel climbing trail, this trail is steep but relatively short so it is not uncommon to see people pushing their bikes up it. For people new to these trails it is recommended to start on Dirty Frank or Waka-Waka, these trails are the only two not accessed from the summit and contain some of the network's easier terrain. All of the other trails start at the summit of Arrowhead. Conway is one of the network's longer trails and contains a very challenging rock roll. Chubbs is one of the shortest trails but its steep lines and countless rollers will be enjoyed by any experienced rider.

Food and Lodging

Since Arrowhead is also located in Claremont, it shares most of its food and lodging options with Moody Park. The Barn Cafe is a great spot for breakfast or lunch and Ramunto's offers tasty pizza and is a good option for a sit-down meal in Claremont. Sunapee, NH is also close to Claremont and offers some fancier options for dining and lodging. For those looking for a hotel in Claremont, the Common Man is a good option and the closest campground would be Wilgus State Park, just across the river in Vermont.

Support Arrowhead

For those looking to donate their time or money to the trails on Arrowhead, contact The Wheel House bike shop as they are the ones who built most of the trails there and are also responsible for maintaining them.
