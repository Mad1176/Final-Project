Aqueduct Trails and Mount Peg

Aqueduct and Mount Peg are two different trail systems in Woodstock, however, they are built and maintained by the same organization so they are condensed into one page for simplicity on this website. These networks have a wide range of trails, from beginner trails to trails designed for riders training for the enduro race series. The trails at Mount Peg do span a larger area than Aqueduct at the moment and are known to contain more climbing, but the riders who brave the climbs will be rewarded with fun and flowy descents. The trails at Aquedecut are slightly newer and the climbs there are less gradual, but the network is currently expanding and still contains some very fun downhills. Mount Peg's trailhead is located at the pull-off for Knox Meadow just past the Woodstock Athletic Center. If you are having trouble finding the trailhead just ask the front desk at the athletic center. The trailhead for Aqueduct is on Cox District Road across from the Cox Reservoir and the trailhead is well-marked with signs.

What to bring?

Since both networks have a wide range of terrain it is possible to ride them on any mountain bike. However, a trail bike would probably be the best choice as there is a lot of climbing at Mount Peg and some of the descents at both networks can be challenging. Some trails at Mount Peg can take you pretty far away from your car so it would be recommended to go prepared with water, food, and tools. Most of the trails at Aqueduct stay a little closer to the car so food and tools are not essential but it is still a good idea to be prepared.

Food and Lodging

The South Woodstock Country Store is very close to Mount Peg and offers great breakfast and lunch sandwiches as well as salads. The Woodstock Farmers Market is very close to Aqueduct Trails and has a cooler of homemade sandwiches as well as great cookies and pastries. The Woodstock Inn is a large hotel in the center of Woodstock. The closest campground would be Quechee State Park in White River Junction, VT.

Support the Trails

The trails at Mount Peg and Aqueduct are both built and managed by WAMBA or Woodstock Area Mountain Bike Association. Please visit their website (WAMBA) if you are intrested in donating your time or money.
